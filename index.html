Frequently Asked Questions

+

What is ecology?

Ecology is the study of interactions between organisms (living things) and their environment (both living and non-living parts).

+

What are the different types of ecosystems?

There are many types, but some common ones include forests, grasslands, deserts, oceans, and wetlands.

+

What is biodiversity?

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth, at all levels, from genes to species to ecosystems.

+

What are biotic factors?

Biotic factors are living things in an ecosystem, like plants, animals, fungi, and bacteria.

+

What are abiotic factors?

Abiotic factors are non-living parts of the environment, like sunlight, temperature, water, air, and soil.

+

What is the role of producers in an ecosystem?

Producers, like plants, capture energy from the sun and use it to produce food (organic matter) through photosynthesis.

+

What are consumers?

Consumers are organisms that eat other organisms to get energy. They can be herbivores (plant-eaters), carnivores (meat-eaters), or omnivores (eat both plants and animals).

+

What is a decomposer?

Decomposers are organisms that break down dead organisms and waste products, returning nutrients back to the ecosystem.

+

What is the carbon cycle?

The carbon cycle is the process of carbon moving through the atmosphere, living organisms, and the soil.

+

What is climate change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperature and weather patterns across the globe, primarily caused by human activities that increase greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.
